What is a SIP Trunk?

SIP stands for Session Initiation Protocol, a technology mechanism used to establish and terminate streaming media services containing voice and data. The 'trunk' element originates from legacy copper networks; it was, and in some cases still is, used to refer to bundles of lines shared between users. SIP Trunks are generally the industry standard for connecting business phone networks over the internet. The technology can facilitate many simultaneous calls and is both cost-effective and flexible.

What is needed to connect to a SIP Trunk?

Internet Connection (or private circuit)

Without an internet connection, the IP functionality of Voice over IP will not work. You need to be connected to your operator's telephone exchange, and via IP, this is generally done over the internet. Phone calls work over the internet connection when using a SIP trunk with an IP PBX, VoIP phone, or SIP client.

What are VoIP Phones / SIP Clients?

These are the devices each user is going to use as a phone. They have to be connected to the exchange. The most commonly used office desk phone devices are VoIP phones. They are similar to regular phones and can be connected directly to the internet or to an on-premise PBX, which then connects either to the internet or to the PSTN TDM network via an SBC.

Why use managed voice services instead of an IP PBX?

Cost savings,When using an IP PBX, the customer or business owner is responsible for setting everything up. Therefore, most enterprises using their own IP PBX hire professionals to do so for maintenance and to solve any issues that may arise. This hidden cost soon accumulates and may negate the savings made by using IP PBX systems in the first place.

As enterprises become larger and systems become more complex, businesses will likely have IT support teams, and often these teams will support the telephony devices and PBXs. Depending on the business model, this could be beneficial, yet for small to medium-sized enterprises, this is rarely the case.

This is the most basic service, often referred to as an A-Z Carrier Trunk. It is suitable for any size of user, from small single-line residential instances to larger call centers and carrier operations.

The main thing that can be said about SIP trunk is that it is generally a marketing term. It refers to an IP voice connection, which can be single or multi-channel and can have various limitations or restrictions depending on the client or supplier needs.

What is NTECH Investments' capacity?

We can currently handle 600 live simultaneous calls at a rate of 100 calls per second per exchange. We have interconnected multiple exchanges in various data centers.

What limits are placed on clients?

Generally, we limit clients to 16 channels and between 5 to 15 calls per second. This avoids congestion of the TDM networks, while our central data centers have the capacity for hundreds of lines and calls per second. However, this is not necessarily true for small businesses or even villages. Copper network bundles still exist in remote destinations, and usually, these are sold in bundles of 16 channels. If all 16 channels were to be occupied at the same time, then the destination becomes blocked. We impose these limits to avoid abuse by call centers. We have special rates for call center traffic, where the limits and tolerances are less imposing. Contact us for details.

Telephone numbers for home and business use.

In many countries, it has been many years since it was necessary to lease a copper line circuit to obtain a telephone number. We offer telephone numbers, also known as DIDs, for business or personal use.

Our numbers are unbundled, which means they can be assigned directly to you. In layman's terms, you don't need a dedicated landline. If you have internet access, then no matter where you are in the world, with a simple piece of software or an internet IP telephone, you can receive calls.

What we refer to here as unbundling is equally compelling. Not only are you not tied to a dedicated circuit such as a copper phone line, but you're also free to solicit outbound telephony from your own SIP trunks (service providers) should their offers be more advantageous than what we can offer.

How this works is we route the calls to your platform, PBX, or IP telephony device, and you answer the calls as they are received. Naturally, it can become more complex with office and carrier systems, so for enterprise, we also offer managed voice services.

Types of Telephone Number

The following are some of the various types of numbers available and their key features.

Fixed Landlinesare telephone numbers with a specific 'fixed' or 'proper' geographical location area code associated with their dial prefix. For example, when dialed using UK dial patterns, the local dial prefix is 0131, or from international origins with the prefix +44131, the number following the prefix is associated with telephone exchanges within Edinburgh, Scotland. Geographical landline numbers are usually termed as fixed numbers, and businesses use them to portray their local presence and facilitate marketing.

Cellular mobile numbers, cellular numbers not normally locked to specific geographical cities. These numbers are typically countrywide. There can be different charge bands applied in calling these depending on commercial terms imposed by the operator or, in some instances, the regulatory bodies.

Satellite numbers are usually universal numbers not georgraphically assocated to particular countries, they are often used as the identifiers of roaming handsets used within aviation and maritime veseels.

Universal numbers are usually universal numbers not geographically associated with particular countries. They are often used as the identifiers of roaming handsets used within aviation and maritime vessels.

IPRN, 'International Premium Rate Number.' is a type of number used by content providers and other enterprises as a method to pay for various services. These offer the holder outpayments rebates which vary from small to large. Different payment rates can exist depending on the origin of traffic and volume of calls.

Domestic Premium Rate numbers are generally considered to be the nationally recognized country-specific premium rate numbers with the highest call costs, and of course, outpayment rates. In the industry, these are sometimes called domestic numbers and shouldn't be confused with proper fixed numbers.

Country 'Wide' or 'NGN' are non-geographic numbers. These service numbers' revenue share, accessibility, and cost to dial vary depending on the particular number range used as well as the source and origin country.

Personal Numbers typically formatted in a similar manner to mobile numbers, these are often charged at higher tariffs. Concepts envisioned the end users using ingress rebate to enable forwarding of the personal number to a mobile number; however, generally, this didn't happen. Where consumers have adopted these numbers, it has generally been for vanity patterns. Some mobile operators have been known to block calls to personal numbers.

VoIP Numbers Special number prefixes meant for exclusive internet use are a legacy from earlier times when DIDs could not be separated from copper TDM services. Generally, these have fallen under the umbrella of 'Wide NGN' numbering, with vanity patterns maintaining popularity.

Special Service Numbers, other numbers that exist are special numbers, like freephone/free call and shared cost. All of these expect the receiver of the call to pay part or all of the charges incurred. These are ideal for businesses wanting to make a good impression and bond with end clients.

Other Number and service code types local regulators will often provide short service codes for specialist services such as live operator assistance, police, fire, coastguard, and so on. Among these are revenue share types such as directory enquiry, or service types such as the speaking clock. Depending on jurisdiction and client requirements, we can facilitate the provision of both the service and number.

SIP URI ENUM numbers bridge the switched traditional networks and the internet by translating the numbers into internet addresses using DNS. These can be privately linked with or without the public domain. The ENUM project is not a new standard; it has been around for a couple of decades. However, it is growing in recognition, and a registry exists which is managed by the ITU-T TSB (International Telecommunications Union, Telephony, Telecommunications Standardization Bureau).
